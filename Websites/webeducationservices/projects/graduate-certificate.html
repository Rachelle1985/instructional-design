Graduate Certificate Program — 6 Modules

A complete, rigor-ready course architecture in Canvas — consistent module design, real assessment, and automated grading that gives faculty time back.

Canvas LMS Higher Education Course architecture & build 12 weeks
The Challenge

Deep expertise, no consistent structure

Faculty subject-matter experts had years of knowledge but no shared instructional structure. Each module looked and behaved differently, assessment was uneven, and grading was eating hours that should have gone to teaching.

The program needed a repeatable course architecture in Canvas — one that held graduate-level rigor, aligned every assessment to learning outcomes, and felt seamless to students moving from module to module.

Project Snapshot

  • ClientUniversity professional graduate program
  • AudienceGraduate students · ~60 per cohort
  • Deliverable6 fully built Canvas modules
  • StandardsAligned to QM principles
The Approach

One template, six rigorous modules

I designed a master module template — consistent navigation, objectives mapped to Bloom's Taxonomy, readings, video lectures, discussion, and assessment in a predictable rhythm — then built all six modules against it. Students never had to relearn how the course worked; they could focus entirely on the content.

Assessment that runs itself

Clear rubrics, structured discussion forums, and Canvas quizzes with automated grade passback meant faculty spent less time on mechanics and more on feedback. An alignment matrix tied every activity back to a program outcome, making the certificate defensible for accreditation review.
