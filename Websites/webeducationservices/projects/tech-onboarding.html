New Employee Orientation — Tech Sector

A responsive, mobile-first orientation that distributed hires actually finish — often before their first day.

Articulate Rise 360 Technology / SaaS Instructional design & build 4 weeks
The Challenge

A remote-first team with a desktop-only orientation

A ~150-person B2B SaaS company was hiring remotely across nine time zones, but its orientation lived in clunky, desktop-only LMS pages that new hires skipped. With roughly 15 hires a quarter, first-week experiences were inconsistent, managers fielded the same questions over and over, and ramp-up time was creeping upward.

They needed an orientation that met people where they actually are — on their phones, in the gaps before day one — and that felt as modern as the product they'd be selling.

Project Snapshot

  • ClientB2B SaaS company · ~150 employees, fully remote
  • AudienceNew hires across 9 time zones (~15/quarter)
  • DeliverableResponsive Rise course + resource hub
  • Format4 microlearning lessons, ~35 min total
The Approach

Mobile-first, bite-sized, and genuinely useful

Rise 360 was the right tool: responsive by default and fast to update as the company evolved. The orientation was broken into short, self-contained lessons — company story, how we work, tools & access, and "your first week" — so a new hire could complete one on a commute and the next over coffee.

A resource hub that outlives day one

Embedded walkthrough videos, interactive blocks, and quick knowledge checks kept it active rather than passive. A built-in resource library meant the course doubled as a reference new hires returned to well past their first week — cutting repeat questions to managers.
